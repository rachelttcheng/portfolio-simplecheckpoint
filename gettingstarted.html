Getting Started

Ah, yes. The age old gym question: how do you actually motivate yourself to start going to the gym?

And then the age old gym answer: Just go! Going to the gym consistently isn't just about motivation -- you need discipline as well.

I'll admit that I don't wake up every day wanting to go to the gym. But I go anyways, and after I'm done with my session I'm always glad I had pushed myself to go. At the same time, be kind to yourself if you miss a day. Results don't happen overnight, but they also won't disappear overnight either!

Here are some things I've found that help with motivating/disciplining me to go to the gym:

1. Bring a friend

Having a gym buddy keeps both of you in check with going to the gym. It's also great for moral support!

2. Wear something that makes you feel comfortable and confident

Never underestimate the importance of
wearing something you look and feel good in to the gym!
I find that wearing a cute workout set makes me excited to go to
the gym and do my thing. Whatever looking and feeling good means to you, pursue that!

I currently wear a lot of things from Gymshark (not surprising).
If you're lifting heavy, I would also recommend wearing some
flat-soled, high top shoes on leg days, since these will help you stay more "connected" to the ground to stabilize your movements and support your ankles.

I currently wear high-top Converse. Some other
popular shoes are Nike Blazers and high-top Vans.

3. Set goals for yourself

Set goals for yourself, and plan out what you're going to do at the gym before you go so you don't get anxious when you're there. Some of my current goals are building my shoulder muscles up to the point where they become no-slip barriers for any bag that I'm wearing, as well as looking as swole as Roger the Kangaroo.

4. Remember you get to have a yummy shake after as a reward :-)

What do you do after a workout? Make sure you get your protein in! There are many yummy shakes you can make. One of my personal favorites is Silk brand chocolate protein milk, which has 20g of protein per serving (1 cup). I add in some chocolate protein powder to get an extra protein boost and enjoy the taste and feeling of knowing that my muscles are being fed well.
